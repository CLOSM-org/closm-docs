On CLOSM i, you can record your fractal art on the blockchain as NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens).

## What is an NFT?

NFTs are technology that proves digital data ownership. Once recorded on the blockchain:

- **Uniqueness**: Proves the artwork is yours
- **Permanence**: Records cannot be altered
- **Transferability**: Can be transferred or sold

## Supported Blockchains

CLOSM i supports:

### Polygon (Recommended)
- Low gas fees
- Environmentally friendly
- Recommended for beginners

### Ethereum
- Most widely adopted
- High liquidity
- Higher gas fees

## Preparing to Mint

### 1. Set Up a Wallet

You need a crypto wallet to mint NFTs.

**MetaMask** is recommended:
1. Install [MetaMask](https://metamask.io)
2. Create a wallet
3. Safely store your seed phrase

### 2. Prepare Gas Fees

Minting requires gas fees:

- **Polygon**: Small amount of MATIC
- **Ethereum**: ETH

### 3. Connect Wallet to CLOSM i

1. Open profile settings
2. Click "Connect Wallet"
3. Approve in MetaMask

## Minting Process

1. Click "Mint NFT" on artwork detail page
2. Select blockchain (Polygon / Ethereum)
3. Approve transaction in wallet
4. Wait for minting to complete

## After Minting

View your NFTs:

- **CLOSM i**: "NFT" badge appears on artwork
- **OpenSea**: View and trade on the marketplace

## Monthly Mint Limits

Limits vary by plan:

| Plan | Monthly Mints |
|------|---------------|
| Free | - |
| Lite | 3 |
| Premium | 30 |

Limits reset monthly.

## Important Notes

- Minted NFTs cannot be undone
- Gas fees fluctuate
- NEVER share your wallet seed phrase

## FAQ

### Q: Can I edit artwork after minting?
A: No. NFT-minted artwork cannot be changed. Review carefully before minting.

### Q: Does it show on other platforms besides OpenSea?
A: Yes. Standard NFT protocols are used, so it displays on compatible marketplaces.
